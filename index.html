Common Questions

Questions Installers
Always Ask First.

Before you apply, here are the answers to the questions every installer asks before their first batch.

What if a lead does not pick up their phone?

Every lead gives their WhatsApp number voluntarily and is told an installer will contact them. If you call within 1 hour of delivery, contact rates are above 70%. If a lead is completely unreachable after 3 attempts in 24 hours, message us and we review it. Leads that fail our 8-gate check are not delivered and not charged.

How does billing work exactly?

Your ads budget goes straight to faceebook. Each time a qualified lead is delivered to your WhatsApp, ₦4,000 is charged. We invoice you by batch of 10 or 5 based on your selection. You can set a monthly lead cap so your spend stays predictable. No lead, no charge ever.

Can I pause or stop at any time?

Yes. There is no lock-in, no minimum commitment, and no cancellation fee. If your team is full or you are on a job, message us and we pause your campaign that day. You only pay for leads that have already been delivered.

What cities do you currently cover?

We currently operate in Lagos, Abuja, Port Harcourt, Ibadan, Benin City, Nasarawa, Makurdi, Imo State and other states in Nigeria. Each city has one exclusive slot. Apply and we confirm if your city is still available. New cities open regularly as demand grows.

What is the ₦1.5M+ budget requirement about?

Every homeowner we deliver has confirmed they have the financial capacity to fund a minimum ₦1.5M solar installation. This is verified through their stated monthly power spend (₦30,000+ per month on PHCN and generator combined). We do not deliver anyone who cannot afford your system.

How long before I start receiving leads?

Once you are confirmed and onboarded, your campaign goes live within 48 hours. Most installers receive their first lead within 3–5 days of campaign launch. After that, leads come in based on Facebook ad performance typically 2–8 per week depending on your city size.

What if the campaign runs for a full week and produces zero qualified leads?

We only charge ₦5,000 per lead delivered, and zero leads delivered means zero charge. Your ad spend went to Facebook for traffic impressions and clicks, that money leaves your hands the moment it hits the ad account, the same as if you ran the ads yourself. What we do when lead volume is low is diagnose the creative and audience targeting, make adjustments, and keep optimising. We have a direct financial incentive to get leads flowing because we only earn when you receive them. We cannot run a charity campaign that produces nothing it costs us the same management time either way.
